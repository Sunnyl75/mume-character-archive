Being the son of Eärendil, Anardil ruled Gondor from the city of Osgiliath as
the sixth in the Line of Kings. Although not many events from his reign have
come to endure in the commemorated history, peace and brotherhood prevailed.
After eighty-seven years his son Ostoher succeeded him and created the custom
of the Kings spending summer in the city now commonly known as Minas Tirith.
